First Martian Picture

The Mariner 4 was launched on November 28, 01964 on a 7¬Ω-month voyage to survey Mars. It performed the first successful flyby of the planet on July 14 and 15, 01965. These were the first images of another planet ever returned from deep space.

Mariner 4 took 21 images of the planet (plus an unusable 22nd). Each photograph took approximately six hours to transmit back to Earth. The total data returned by the mission was 5.2 million bits (about 634 kB).

While the NASA team waited for the data to be digitized into images, they printed each pixel value and started to color them in by hand to confirm the data was making sense. This was the first image from Mars.
