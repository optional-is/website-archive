Torpenhow Hill

Torpenhow Hill is a made-up term based on a hill near the village of Torpenhow in Cumbria, England.

It is an example of a tautological placename. In Old Saxons torr is hill. In Welsh pen is also hill, and in Old Norse haugr, which became 'how' is also 'hill'. Therefore, the name 'Torpenhow Hill' can be translated as: hill-hill-hill Hill.
